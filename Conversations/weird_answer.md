**Homemade Chicken Broth Recipe**

Ingredients:

* 2-3 lbs chicken bones (you can use raw or cooked bones, or a combination of both)
* 4-6 carrots, chopped
* 2-3 celery stalks, chopped
* 2 cloves of garlic, minced
* 1 large onion, chopped
* 6-8 cups of water
* Optional: herbs and spices (e.g., thyme, bay leaves, black pepper)

Instructions:

1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. Place the chicken bones on a baking sheet and roast them in the oven for 30 minutes to 1 hour, or until they're nicely browned. This step will enhance the flavor of your broth.
3. In a large pot (at least 6-quart capacity), combine the roasted chicken bones, chopped carrots, celery, garlic, and onion.
4. Pour in the water and bring the mixture to a boil over high heat.
5. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer the broth for 6-24 hours. Yes, you read that right - 6-24 hours! The longer you simmer, the more flavorful your broth will be.
6. After the simmering time has passed, remove the pot from the heat and let it cool slightly.
7. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a clean container. Discard the solids.
8. Refrigerate or freeze the broth for later use.

**Tips and Variations:**

* Use a slow cooker: If you prefer, you can also make chicken broth in a slow cooker. Simply brown the bones and then transfer everything to the slow cooker with some water and cook on low for 8-12 hours.
* Add acidity: A squeeze of fresh lemon juice or a splash of vinegar can help balance the flavors in your broth.
* Spice it up: Experiment with different herbs and spices, such as peppercorns, bay leaves, or dried thyme, to create unique flavor profiles.
* Make it clear: If you prefer a clear broth, you can skim off any impurities that rise to the surface after straining.
